Energy in Australia

A visualisation by Albert Arifin

Australia is one of the most cultural country in the world, and home to 25.69 million people. It has an area of 7.688 million kilometers squared and is the sixth biggest country on earth. With that many people living in the country, the lifestyle can vary. Now, the following article will breakdown the country into the seven states of the country, and see how the electricity usage of the people in respective states are diffrent. This will be examined from the diffrent type of fuels it uses, the GSP per capita, and more.

Energy Consumption Rate (shown in Map)

The following map shows the energy consumption of each state from the year 2016 to 2020. All figures are under the unit of 'GJ per person' (giga joule per person). Observing the map can tell us just which state has the highest gigajoule per capita and how it may have changed positively or negatively in the span of four years.

Renewable vs Non-Renewable Energy Usage (shown in pie chart)

The following pie chart shows the renewable and non-renewable energy consumption in Australia from the year 2008 to 2020. All figures are under the unit of PJ, petajoule, which equal to 1 000 000 000 000 000 (10^15) joules. Observing the pie chart and using the slider can tell us how people had shift to using renewable sources of energy over time. This shows positive change in people's habits and that it can mean there are progress to eliminate global warming, which involves using more renewable energy sources.
